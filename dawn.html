dawn

Back to home Sept 2023

    Today’s my birthday. I ought to watch the sunrise.

    One thought was all it took to upend my failing attempts to fall asleep. Suddenly the pursuit as well as the need for rest vanished. I rose from my covers, overcome not with drive or motivation per se, but rather intention—intention to watch the dawn of my seventeenth year of life.

    This was my first summer where I was truly on my own, where I had to choose how to spend each minute of every day, unlike previous summers spent mostly between camp and vacation.

    I filled my days with what was familiar: trashy television with friends, viola sonatas, deadlifts, modal jams, and Camus.

    However, there was a subtle but vital missing piece. I pursued what I enjoyed and was important to me, but I lacked a certain something: deliberate consciousness. My daily life was a routine of habit with little else.

    Yet on this particular night or morning, depending on perspective, I was reminded of the necessity for deliberate consciousness in my life through that one thought, that one intention, to watch the sunrise.

    I made a pot of green tea; its aroma, taste, and texture served to ground me in the present moment. I looked out my living room window, the semi-orange hue of my porch at the bottom of my peripheral vision disrupting the near black of the earliest hours of morning. I stepped out, putting down the tea and putting on the soundtrack to the TV show Sonny Boy. I ritualistically reserve this album for when I feel an approaching moment of great personal change, such as this one. It is a multi-genre masterpiece ranging from sparse, ambient folktronica soundscapes to dense, mechanically flowing math rock. This constant switching between master works of contrasting genres reliably evokes within me feelings of bitter sweetness, comfortable uncertainty, and joyous impermanence that enhance moments such as these.

    I stood on my porch, leaning forward against the railing, looking out into the impenetrable woods past my backyard, a slice of unspoiled nature close to home. I could feel, with each sip of tea, its heat flowing down from my mouth, to throat, to chest, to stomach, the subtle yet soothing aroma in my nose and mouth. I could also taste a slight astringency on my tongue—must’ve been steeped too long. I hadn’t noticed it earlier, but the darkness that welcomed me onto the porch had begun to fade. I could see the sun’s light creep over the roof behind me; I forgot that my porch faces west. I turned away from the porch railing so I could see the sun’s arrival. It brought a host of wonders with it: a warmth that reached out and embraced me, color that washed over my surroundings, turning subtle differences in shade into vibrant contrast, and of course, itself, big ball of yellow radiance, light bringer and life giver. I’ve watched many sunrises, often with less obscured views, but regardless, the sight never ceases to amaze.

    With sun in my eyes, tea in my belly, and music in my ears, a single thought arrives:

    This moment is nice. Enjoy it.
